About the Doctor
"From Kids to Adults, we're here to take care of every smile at ALL AGES."

Dr. Margot Gail Marquez Ilagan, the owner and head dentist of Dr. Margot Gail M. Ilagan Dental Clinic, is a dedicated and highly skilled dental professional committed to providing quality oral healthcare.

She completed her degree in Doctor of Dental Medicine at Centro Escolar University (CEU) — one of the leading institutions in the Philippines known for excellence in dental education — and built a strong foundation through both academic excellence and hands-on clinical experience.

Known for her gentle approach and commitment to patient comfort, Dr. Ilagan embodies CEU's core values of competence, commitment, and compassion in every aspect of her practice. She continuously enhances her knowledge by attending seminars and training to stay updated with modern dental techniques.

Dr. Ilagan Believes In
  • Delivering high-quality dental care
  • Building trust with patients
  • Promoting long-term oral health
  • Creating a comfortable and friendly clinic environment

A passionate dental professional whose dedication and expertise make her a valuable contributor to the field of dentistry — positively impacting the lives of many, one smile at a time.

Our Services

Comprehensive
dental care.

From preventive treatments to advanced restorations — all under one roof, delivered by a caring team.

01
Tooth Extraction
Why it's Needed
Damaged, decayed, or crowded teeth can cause pain and infection if left untreated.
How it's Done
The tooth is loosened from its socket using dental instruments and gently removed under local anesthesia.
02
Tooth Restoration
Why it's Needed
Cavities and broken teeth weaken your bite and can worsen without treatment.
How it's Done
Decayed portions are removed and the tooth is rebuilt using composite resin, amalgam, or other materials.
03
Oral Prophylaxis
Why it's Needed
Plaque and tartar buildup leads to gum disease and cavities if not regularly cleaned.
How it's Done
A dental scaler removes deposits above and below the gum line, followed by polishing the tooth surfaces.
04
Dentures
Why it's Needed
Missing teeth affect chewing, speech, and facial structure over time.
How it's Done
Custom-fitted removable appliances are crafted from impressions of your mouth to replace missing teeth.
05
Jacket Crown / Fixed Bridge
Why it's Needed
Severely damaged or missing teeth need structural support to restore function and appearance.
How it's Done
A crown caps a damaged tooth; a bridge uses adjacent teeth as anchors to fill a gap with a prosthetic tooth.
06
Veneers
Why it's Needed
Stained, chipped, or uneven teeth affect your smile's appearance and confidence.
How it's Done
Thin porcelain or composite shells are bonded to the front surface of teeth after minimal enamel preparation.
07
Orthodontic Treatment / Braces
Why it's Needed
Misaligned teeth and bite issues can cause jaw pain, uneven wear, and difficulty cleaning.
How it's Done
Brackets and wires apply gentle, continuous pressure to move teeth into proper alignment over time.
08
Retainers
Why it's Needed
After braces, teeth can shift back without a retainer to hold them in position.
How it's Done
A custom-fitted appliance — fixed or removable — keeps teeth in their corrected position post-treatment.
09
Fluoride Application
Why it's Needed
Fluoride strengthens enamel and helps prevent early-stage tooth decay.
How it's Done
A concentrated fluoride gel, foam, or varnish is applied directly to the teeth for a few minutes.
10
Teeth Whitening
Why it's Needed
Stains from food, drinks, and aging can discolor enamel and dull your smile.
How it's Done
A professional-grade bleaching agent is applied to the teeth, often activated with light or heat for faster results.
11
Wisdom Tooth Removal / Odontectomy
Why it's Needed
Impacted or partially erupted wisdom teeth can cause pain, crowding, and infection.
How it's Done
The tooth is surgically exposed, sectioned if needed, and removed under local or general anesthesia.
12
Frenectomy, Gingivectomy, Operculectomy
Why it's Needed
Excess gum tissue or tight frenum attachments can restrict movement or cause gum disease.
How it's Done
Minor surgical procedures remove or reposition soft tissue using a scalpel or laser for quicker healing.
13
Root Canal Treatment
Why it's Needed
Infected or inflamed tooth pulp causes severe pain and can spread to surrounding bone.
How it's Done
The pulp is removed, the canals are cleaned and shaped, then sealed with a filling and crowned for protection.
14
Digital Periapical X-Ray
Why it's Needed
X-rays reveal hidden decay, bone loss, and root issues invisible to the naked eye.
How it's Done
A small digital sensor is placed in the mouth and a low-radiation beam captures a detailed image instantly.
